# Data Quality Summary

As part of the Day 1 data exploration and validation process, all available datasets were successfully loaded into the project environment and examined to understand their structure, quality, and completeness. The primary objective was to ensure that the data is reliable and suitable for building the Mutual Fund Analytics Platform.

A total of **15 datasets** were analyzed, including 10 provided datasets and 5 live NAV datasets collected through API integration. Together, these datasets contain more than **96,000 records**, covering various aspects of the mutual fund industry such as fund information, NAV history, assets under management (AUM), SIP inflows, investor transactions, portfolio holdings, benchmark indices, and scheme performance metrics.

The **Fund Master dataset**, which serves as the foundation of the project, contains **40 mutual fund schemes** and **15 columns** of scheme-level information. The dataset includes important attributes such as AMFI codes, fund houses, categories, sub-categories, benchmark indices, expense ratios, fund managers, and risk classifications. The analysis identified **10 major fund houses**, including SBI Mutual Fund, HDFC Mutual Fund, ICICI Prudential MF, Nippon India MF, Kotak Mahindra MF, Axis Mutual Fund, Aditya Birla Sun Life MF, UTI Mutual Fund, Mirae Asset MF, and DSP Mutual Fund. The schemes are classified into two major categories—**Equity** and **Debt**—with further segmentation into sub-categories such as Large Cap, Small Cap, Mid Cap, Flexi Cap, ELSS, Corporate Bond, Gilt, Short Duration, and Banking & PSU Debt.

The data quality assessment revealed excellent results. The Fund Master dataset contains **40 records and 15 columns**, with **zero missing values** and **zero duplicate records**. Similarly, the NAV History dataset contains **46,000 records**, the Investor Transactions dataset contains **32,778 records**, and the Benchmark Indices dataset contains **8,050 records**, all of which were loaded successfully without any missing values or duplicate entries.

Several validation checks were performed, including missing value analysis, duplicate record detection, data type verification, and file integrity checks. The results showed that almost all datasets are fully complete and consistent. No duplicate records were found in any dataset, and all files were read successfully without corruption or formatting issues.

The only exception was the **Monthly SIP Inflows dataset**, which contains **48 records and 6 columns**. During validation, **12 missing values** were identified in the **Year-over-Year Growth (YoY Growth %) column**. After reviewing the data, these missing values were determined to be expected because YoY growth calculations require data from the previous year, which was not available for the initial months. Therefore, these missing values are considered valid and do not impact the overall quality of the dataset.

The analysis also confirmed that all key identifier fields are available and populated correctly. In particular, the **AMFI Code** field is present for every scheme and will serve as the primary linking key across multiple datasets, including Fund Master, NAV History, Scheme Performance, Portfolio Holdings, Investor Transactions, and Live NAV data. This consistency will simplify database design and ETL development in later project phases.

In addition to the provided datasets, live NAV data was successfully retrieved through the mfapi.in API for five schemes: SBI Bluechip Fund, ICICI Bluechip Fund, Nippon Large Cap Fund, Axis Bluechip Fund, and Kotak Bluechip Fund. The downloaded datasets contained between **3,251 and 3,580 historical NAV records each**, with no missing values or duplicate records observed.

Overall, the data quality assessment indicates that the datasets are highly reliable, well-structured, and ready for analytical processing. Out of more than **96,000 records analyzed**, only **12 expected missing values** were identified, and no duplicate records were found. The consistency of the data structure, completeness of critical fields, and successful validation checks provide a strong foundation for the next stages of the project, including data cleaning, ETL pipeline development, database creation, star schema implementation, and dashboard analytics.

The Day 1 validation process confirms that the available datasets provide a strong and dependable foundation for building a comprehensive Mutual Fund Analytics Platform.
